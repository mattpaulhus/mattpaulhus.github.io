Leading Quality of Life in Poland

Leading and serving a team responsible for quality of life in Poland

August 2020 Matt Paulhus

Our Mission

Maximize the quality of life of Soldiers living on any of the five Żagań Cluster Forward Operating Sites to relieve the responsibility of base operating support functions from existing tenants and develop base operations continuity and innovation.

How

Leading a team of 12 U.S. Army Reserve leaders, including 5 direct reports, understand the capabilities of each of our assigned bases in order to enable the missions of the tenant units. Enable each of the team members to work at their personal bests, knowing that this would allow them, most at remote off-site bases, to then take care of the U.S. Army service members living on the bases. Form a team.

Result

Tenants living on any of the five Żagań Cluster bases had a much higher quality of life compared to other bases in Poland. While the list of physical updates is numerous, including dining updates, new mattresses, gym updates, facilities, etc., I believe it is the building of a strong Poland, knowledge continuity, positive experience for tenant service members, and enduring relationships that resulted from that was most important.

What I learned

How to extend a positive influence on teams using emotional intelligence and how to help others work at their personal bests. The importance of 1-on-1s. The importance of mindfulness in all situations. Dealing with conflict. Working with diverse work styles and perspectives. Finally, the opportunity to practice servant leadership.
